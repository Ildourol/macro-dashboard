2. Volatility Risk Premium implied vs. realized, skill-derived estimate

Methodology (rebuilt 2026-07-06): volatility risk premium is the premium of implied over realized: VRP = IV - RV. Realized volatility is now computed close-to-close from a proper daily S&P 500 price history (FMP feed) rather than the earlier six hand-entered moves: sample stdev of log returns × √252. Two windows: 10-day RV = 12.3% (σ≈0.77%/day, last two calmer weeks) and ~4-week RV = 15.4% (σ≈0.97%/day, 18 obs, includes the late-June chop and the 07/02 rotation). Implied side uses VIX 15.81 (07/02 close) / 16.34 (live 07/06). CORRECTION — the prior page reported RV ≈ 8.5% from a 6-observation window during the market's calmest stretch, which overstated VRP badly; the wider, properly sampled windows are the honest measure.

Implied vs. Realized Volatility (annualized %)

How to read: bars compare what the market is paying for future volatility (VIX) against what the index has actually delivered (realized) over two windows, with the VIX 50D average as regime context. The gap between an RV bar and the VIX bar is the volatility risk premium. Current read: VIX 15.81 vs 10-day RV 12.3% = VRP +3.6 pts (near the ~4pt long-run S&P norm); vs 4-week RV 15.4% = VRP +0.4 pts (below norm). Either way the premium is roughly fair, not the "+7.3 rich" this page previously showed. Use: the generic edge in selling S&P premium has largely closed; owning targeted convexity across the Jul 14 CPI / Jul 15 PPI window is close to fairly priced and preferable to blanket premium-selling — especially with VIX ticking up to 16.3 live.

What changed and why it matters INFERENCE: the earlier page argued options were "rich vs realized" because dealer long-gamma was crushing realized vol. With realized measured over a full month — capturing the late-June selloff and the 07/02 rotation — delivered vol is actually running 12–15%, right up against implied. So VRP is fair-to-thin, not fat. The practical consequence: this desk should stop treating premium-selling as a free carry trade here and instead lean on defined-risk directional expressions (long YM / short-or-hedged NQ per the playbook) with convexity owned through the CPI window. If the gamma regime breaks post-CPI, realized snaps higher and even today's thin premium proves too cheap — which is exactly when owned convexity pays.
