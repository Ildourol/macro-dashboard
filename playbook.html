Retail Futures Playbook & Bottom Line

Operating rules plus final cross-dashboard synthesis in one page.

1. Operating Rules

1. Read the divergence first, the index second. The Dow and Nasdaq moved in opposite directions by more than a full percentage point this week. When you see this, the trade is the spread (long YM / reduced NQ exposure), not a directional bet on "the market." The CME volume data confirms this isn't noise MNQ retail volume (3.99M contracts) dwarfs every other equity-index product, meaning the Nasdaq weakness is a crowded-long unwind, not random chop.

2. Let the bond market set your equity risk appetite. The bear-steepening curve (see Rates page) is the macro engine behind this week's rotation. Before entering any small-cap (RTY) or growth (NQ) long, check whether the 10Y yield is stabilizing. The Treasury options call/put skew flip (42.8%59.5% calls in one week) is an early signal worth monitoring for a change in that engine.

3. Own convexity into the catalyst — but know it is fairly priced now, not a giveaway. The rebuilt VRP (see Executive Risk Lab) shows S&P index options are close to FAIR versus delivered vol (VIX 15.81–16.34 vs realized 12–15%), not the "+7.3 rich" the terminal previously claimed. So the edge is no longer in selling premium; it is in owning a small, defined-risk hedge into the Jul 14 CPI while it is cheap in absolute terms and while the huge cross-sectional dispersion (Tech −2.9% vs Healthcare +2.6% on 07/02) argues a correlation break is a live risk the headline VIX underprices.

4. Do not flatten all COT reads into one equity-index call. The revamped Insider Week COT page shows a split tape: S&P 500 and Dow E-mini sit at 0% Commercial COT Index, while Nasdaq is 80% and Russell is 74%. Treat COT as a cross-index filter, not a blanket bullish/bearish label.

5. Size for the stop, not for the setup. Every setup on the Trade Setups page lists a technical invalidation level. Use micro contracts (MES/MNQ/MYM/M2K/MGC) to size dollar risk to 12% of account equity per idea.

6. Treat "flight to safety" skeptically this cycle. Falling dollar + rising long-end yields is not the standard risk-off signature (see Rates page and Strategist Roundup). If equities correct from here, don't assume long bonds and a bid dollar will behave as the reliable hedge they have historically been.

2. Bottom Line Synthesis INFERENCE

Most likely outcome INFERENCE: Continued bear-steepening drives further rotation from long-duration growth (NQ) into value/cyclicals (YM), with the S&P 500 headline holding up while internals churn hard beneath the surface. Merrill's own base case (55% probability, see Strategist Roundup) supports this resilient fundamentals, double-digit earnings growth, broadening participation while their bull case has been raised to 25% on stronger earnings revisions.

Primary systemic risk to retail capital: A compressed VIX (15.81 close, 16.34 live 07/06) is masking a real correlation-break risk — realized S&P vol is actually running 12–15% (not the ~8.5% this terminal once showed), so the index has been moving more than the calm VIX implies. Persistent put-buying across every index, every session (see Options Activity) shows the smart-money hedging community already sees it. Retail portfolios that are "diversified" only via a total-market index are carrying concentrated mega-cap/AI momentum exposure (confirmed by MNQ's 3.99M-contract dominance on the Volume & OI page) that the VIX is underpricing.

Highest-conviction tactical trade: Long YM/MYM on confirmed breakout above 528.3, financed with cheap SPX/ES downside convexity while VIX sits sub-16 and dealer gamma remains long (see SPX Gamma page).

Trap to avoid: Buying the NQ/MNQ dip on the assumption this week's tech weakness is routine. It is mechanically linked to the bond-market bear-steepener (see Rates page) and to a genuinely crowded retail futures position (MNQ volume) that combination does not resolve until the long end of the curve stabilizes and that specific crowding unwinds.

What would change this view: A 10Y yield reversal back below 4.40%, a Treasury-options call/put skew that holds above 55% for a second consecutive session, or the next Insider Week COT/Heatmap refresh showing S&P and Dow commercial indexes recovering out of 0% extremes would each argue for re-risking into broader equity exposure.
