BTC Futures Daily Volume vs. Open Interest (mid-to-late June)

How to read: Blue bars = daily BTC futures volume (left axis); amber line = open interest (right axis). The relationship matters more than either alone: volume up + OI up = new positions (conviction); volume up + OI down = liquidation (positions closing). Current read: The 6/24 volume spike (26,086) was followed by OI falling from 22,227 to 20,382 - the textbook liquidation signature; the crowd was exiting, not accumulating. Use: Until the amber line turns back up, treat every BTC bounce as short-covering relief rather than a new uptrend - size accordingly.
What this means INFERENCE: Volume spiking to 26,086 (6/24) while OI subsequently fell from 22,227 to 20,382 is the classic liquidation signature positions being closed, not opened. Combined with the 13.87bps execution friction (vs. sub-1bp on ES), this quantifies why the BTC setup demands wider stops and smaller size: you pay ~14bps just to enter and exit a 5-lot, and the OI trend says the linear-futures crowd is de-risking, not accumulating. Bounces without OI rebuild are lower-conviction by construction.
